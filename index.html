Time for Something New

  • SMW is powerful and unique, has issues
  • Usability of Notion, Airtable, etc
  • SMW 20 years old, Wikibase ~15
  • Many lessons learned

Many of you know the power of Semantic MediaWiki. It's a unique software that is highly flexible and can serve many use cases.

You also know it has some big issues. Despite all its promise, SMW never saw breakthrough success outside the MediaWiki world.

The competition hasn't stood still, and now highly popular tools like Notion and Airtable blow SMW out of the water when it comes to usability.

I've been maintaining SMW for over 15 years and created large parts of Wikibase. So I can say with some authority that we've learned many lessons along the way, and that it is time for something new.

Philosophy
Focus on value, not features

Introducing NeoWiki, a modern solution for structured data, in the form of an open source MediaWiki extension.

You get radically better usability, while we achieve step-change improvements in sustainability.

We are explicitly not aiming for feature parity with prior solutions. You can get 90% of the value from 10% of the features. Thus, we focus on the most common and valuable use cases, giving you a polished and focused experienced.

Comparison one-liners to weave in during demo:

Schemas: The schema enforces types automatically. No more hoping editors get it right.

Forms: Built-in, derived from the schema. Not a separate extension you bolt on.

Views/queries: Think #ask, but with a real query language and first-class result components.

Storage: JSON in revision slots. Versionable, standard, no rebuilds.
